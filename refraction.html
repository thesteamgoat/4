Refraction

Refraction describes how light bends as it passes through one medium to another, such as through glass, air, and water.  Light travels at different speeds through different types of matter, and this is what causes the light to bend.  Consider a straw or pencil placed in a glass of water; it appears to be broken when viewed through the water because the speed of the light slows down in the water.  Water, thick glass, prisms, convex and concave lenses, and eyeglasses all refract light.
